About the Polar Bear

Polar bears (Ursus maritimus) live across the Arctic region and are superbly adapted to cold: thick fur, a layer of insulating fat, and large paws for swimming and walking on ice. They are top predators that primarily hunt seals at breathing holes or from the ice edge.

As sea ice changes seasonally, polar bears travel great distances. Scientists monitor populations closely due to habitat loss and shifts in ice coverage. Humans can reduce impacts by limiting greenhouse gas emissions and protecting Arctic ecosystems.
