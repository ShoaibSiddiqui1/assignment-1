About the Lion

Lions are large carnivorous cats native to Africa and a small population in India. They are social, living in groups called prides, and are known for their loud roars and distinctive manes (in males). Lions typically inhabit grasslands and open woodlands where prey such as zebra and antelope are common.

Lions (Panthera leo) are the only truly social big cats. Lionesses usually hunt cooperatively while males defend territory. Conservation concerns include habitat loss and conflict with humans; several populations are considered vulnerable.

Fast facts

  • Average male weight: 150–250 kg; female: 120–182 kg
  • Roar can be heard up to 8 km (5 miles)
  • Lifespan: ~10–14 years in the wild
